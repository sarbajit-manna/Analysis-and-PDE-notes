# Research Proposal: Geometric-PDE Framework for Moisture Transport in Cellulose Fiber Networks

This proposal was developed during my PhD studies at IISc while exploring mathematical models for moisture transport in cellulose fibre networks.

**Date:** November 2025  
**Context:** Exchange application to CERAMATHS, Université Polytechnique Hauts-de-France (UPHF)

## Important Note

This is a **historical research proposal** prepared in November 2025. It is **not** a current research proposal for PhD applications. It is shared here to document the development of the underlying mathematical ideas and the evolution of the project through discussions with an external researcher.

## Overview

The proposal develops a mathematical framework for moisture transport in cellulose fiber networks, moving from an initial graph-theoretic model to a PDE-based approach using:

- BV spaces for handling intensity jumps and singularities;
- Sobolev spaces for weak formulations and regularity;
- Weighted Sobolev spaces for boundary-layer transport;
- Hardy inequalities for derivative regularity near boundaries;
- Analytic regularity for polyhedral domains with singularities;
- Transmission problems for fiber-fiber interfaces.

## Connection to Prof. Serge Nicaise

The mathematical framework developed in this proposal was strongly influenced by Prof. Serge Nicaise’s work on weighted Sobolev spaces, analytic regularity, and transmission problems.

Prof. Nicaise read the proposal, requested a revised version with additional mathematical clarification, and discussed several aspects of the proposed framework by email. A potential research collaboration was explored but did not proceed because of his retirement in 2026.

## Why This Matters

This proposal illustrates:

- formulation of an interdisciplinary research problem using PDE methods;
- motivation for weighted Sobolev spaces arising from geometric singularities;
- development of mathematical ideas through external feedback and revision;
- progression from graph-based modelling toward analysis and geometric partial differential equations.
