# Riemann Integral and Outer Measure

**Course:** Analysis II (MA222), Spring 2026  
**Instructor:** Prof. Harish Seshadri  
**Primary References:**
- Sheldon Axler, *Measure, Integration & Real Analysis*
- Gerald B. Folland, *Real Analysis*

This folder contains my personal exposition of the Riemann integral and outer measure, written while studying graduate measure theory during Spring 2026.

## Contents

The document develops the transition from classical integration to modern measure theory through the following topics:

1. Riemann integral: partitions, lower and upper sums, integrability, and the failure of the Riemann integral (Dirichlet function).
2. Outer measure: definition, intuition, and fundamental properties including monotonicity, translation invariance, and countable subadditivity.
3. The Heine–Borel theorem and its role in establishing the outer measure of intervals.
4. Non-additivity of outer measure and the motivation for Lebesgue measure.
5. Selected exercises from Chapter 2A of Axler with detailed solutions and conceptual commentary.

## Features

- Detailed mathematical explanations
- Conceptual interpretations and geometric intuition
- Original analogies (for example, the "packing a gift" analogy for outer measure)
- Personal solutions and structural interpretations of selected exercises
- Fully typeset in LaTeX
